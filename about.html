Research experiences

My current research area and master thesis are primarily focused on influential node identification in complex networks.
I proposed two novel centrality measures.
  • One is leveraging concepts of the Laplacian matrix of a graph and Tsallis entropy to quantify a complex system's key nodes. Main idea is evaluating the node importance by removing the node and calculate the change of the network and connecting these process from the perspective of information theory, using entropy to grade for nodes.
    The title is Influential nodes identification by Tsallis entropy and Laplacian centrality in complex networks, and has been submitted to Journal of Computational Science
  • Another is mocking the spreading probability referred to SIR model, stimulating the process of community voting and spreading of trust chains, and seperately calculating each node's score to quantify the importance.
    The title is Trust probability and voting decision to determine the key node in complex networks, and has been submitted to Journal of Informetrics
    • These measures have strengthened my ability to program, data analysis, and collaboration.
